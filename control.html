Privacy as Control

You have a say over what information about you is collected, shared, and used—and for what purposes.

Definition

On the control view, privacy is the ability to manage personal information: grant, deny, or withdraw permissions, and set limits on downstream uses.

Examples

  • App settings that let you opt out of targeted ads or data sharing.
  • Consent forms specifying what data a study can collect and how long it’s kept.
  • “Right to be forgotten” requests that remove stale profiles from a platform.

Why it matters

  • Agency: Treats people as decision-makers, not data sources.
  • Context: What’s okay in one setting may not be okay in another.
  • Accountability: Permissions create obligations others must respect.

Creative element

Quick take: If you can’t set the rules or change your mind later, control is weak—even if your data isn’t public.
