Privacy as Restricted Access

Privacy here means limiting access to yourself—your body, your home, your devices, and your records.

Definition

To be private is to be shielded from intrusion. Barriers—doors, passwords, encryption—mark zones of permitted and forbidden entry.

Examples

  • Locked rooms or “do not disturb” signs.
  • Phone passcodes, two-factor authentication, and end-to-end encryption.
  • Medical portals that require authorization to view your chart.

Why it matters

  • Safety: Keeps bad actors out—physically and digitally.
  • Boundaries: Lets you separate roles (home, school, work).
  • Rest: Solitude enables focus, recovery, and creativity.
