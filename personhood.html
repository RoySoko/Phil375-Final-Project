Privacy & Personhood

Privacy protects the conditions for becoming and remaining a self: autonomy, dignity, and the freedom to experiment.

Definition

On the personhood view, privacy is valuable because it enables self-authorship—space to form beliefs, try identities, and make decisions without pressure or exposure.

Examples

  • Private journaling and therapy notes.
  • Trying out opinions in small groups before going public.
  • Voting in secret ballots to reduce coercion.

Why it matters

  • Autonomy: Decisions feel like yours when made without surveillance.
  • Dignity: Respecting boundaries treats people as ends, not tools.
  • Development: Everyone needs room to change their mind.
