Privacy & Intimacy

Privacy sustains trust in close relationships and protects who you meet, love, and organize with.

Definition

Intimacy views privacy as relationship-centered: we need protected zones to share selectively and to form groups without outsider pressure.

Examples

  • Private group chats and invite-only communities.
  • Confidential counseling or pastoral conversations.
  • Protection for union organizing or advocacy groups.

Why it matters

  • Trust: People share honestly when the circle is safe.
  • Freedom: Association without surveillance supports democracy.
  • Care: Sensitive topics need controlled audiences.
