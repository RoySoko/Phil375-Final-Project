Privacy as Proprietary Interest

On this view, personal information is something you can own or license, similar to property.

Definition

Proprietary theories treat data about you as an asset with rights to exclude, transfer, and seek remedies if others take it without permission.

Examples

  • Licensing your photo for a campaign, not granting full ownership.
  • Seeking compensation after unauthorized commercial use of your data.
  • Contract terms that restrict re-sale of customer lists.

Why it matters

  • Remedies: Property models fit well with damages and enforcement.
  • Clarity: Ownership lines can be easier to manage in markets.
  • Limits: But not all privacy harms are “for sale.”

Creative element

Quick take: Property talk helps with enforcement, but privacy isn’t only a marketplace issue.
