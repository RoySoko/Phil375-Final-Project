Privacy as Secrecy

Privacy here is about keeping certain facts unknown to others.

Definition

Secrecy focuses on concealment: if a detail is not exposed, it remains private—even if you didn’t actively manage permissions.

Examples

  • Withholding your location from public posts.
  • Using pseudonyms in online forums to separate identities.
  • Sealed records or confidential settlement terms.

Why it matters

  • Selective sharing: Not everything should be searchable.
  • Reputation: Some facts can be true but unfairly harmful.
  • Power balance: Secrets limit surveillance advantages.
